ABOUT

I am an anthropologist with broad interests that originate in questions about cultural adaptation to climate change and the evolution of human subsistence ecology.

My main focus is community-based research in the Canadian Arctic, working with the communities of Kangiqsujuaq and Ulukhaktok (with Peter Collings). Our current project, Sanguatsiniq, focuses on Inuit concepts of stress, stress management and its social aspects, as well as on food security and the interaction of the cash and subsistence economies in Arctic settlements. My work employs mixed-methods ranging from network analysis to grounded theory. I have been working in Kangiqsujuaq since 2011 and have spent over 19 months living in the community.

I also have ongoing research collaborations focused on evolutionary-ecological theory and climate change adaptation (with James Holland Jones), zooarchaeology and Neandertal foraging behaviours (with Eugène Morin), and network data collection and analysis methods (especially with Eleanor Power).

For more details, download my curriculum vitae.
